        How to Play

        Oubliette is a conversation. You describe what your character thinks, says, and does — and an AI Dungeon Master narrates the world's reply. There are no menus to fight; just tell the story together.

        Talking to the DM

        Type in the first person, as your character: "I push open the chapel door and call out for the priest." You can speak, act, ask questions, or describe a plan. The DM picks up from there. When in doubt, just say what you do next.

        Out-of-character

        Need to ask a real question — a rule, a recap, a "wait, what's in my pack?" Flip the OOC toggle by the message box. Out-of-character notes are table talk: the DM answers you directly without advancing the story.

        Dice & checks

        When an outcome is uncertain, the DM calls for a check and the game rolls the dice for you — your character's real bonuses are applied automatically. You'll see the roll appear as a chip. The code owns the numbers; the DM only decides when a roll matters and narrates the result.

        Your character

        Open the ☰ menu for everything about your party: the Party Sheets (abilities, skills, AC, spells — all derived for you), your Inventory (equip and unequip gear), and the Bestiary (a reference of the creatures you may meet).

        Growing stronger

        The DM awards experience as you adventure. Watch the XP bar on your character card — when it fills, a Level Up button lights up. Leveling is yours to take when you're ready.

        Setting the table

        When you start a new campaign you'll set the table: a tone dial (from whimsical to grim) and any Lines & Veils — content the story should never cross (a Line) or fade tactfully past (a Veil). You can revisit all of it any time under Settings. The DM honors it.

        Saving your game

        Your campaign saves itself continuously — there's a single ongoing save. Continue from the start menu picks up exactly where you left off. New Game begins a fresh world and replaces the old one, so finish or let go before you start anew.

        That's all you need. The rest you'll discover by playing — which is the whole point. Have a good session.
